Wee Wee Pad Dispenser

Given the details of the device in the patent, I had to design and build a working prototype to demonstrate the fesability of an automated house breaking pad dispensor. The project started with brainstorming ideas on a piece of paper, and grew into the fabrication of a functioning prototype. Along the way I learned a ton, from figuring out what a microcontroller is to building molds and working with composites. It was by no means easy but a challenge I would accept over and over.

Robockey

The final project in my Mechatronics class, consisted of building an autonomous team of hockey playing robots. My three teammates and I had three weeks to design, build, and code three robots to compete in the tournament. They were built entirely from scratch; the chassis was designed and built using 3D printing and laser cutting, the circuits were designed and soldered by hand, and the code was written from scratch. My main role on the team, was to handle the construction of all the circuitry. I also had a hand in the mechanical design of the bots. This was one of the hardest and most advanced projects I have ever been involved in. To the right you can see a beautiful example of all three robots coming together and working as designed.

Sterling Engine

In my Machine Design & Manufacturing class, you spend an entire semester machining a stirling heat engine. Every piece is machined out of aluminum, brass and steel using lathes and mills. You learn how to operate industrial machinery but more importantly you learn how things are manufactured. This gives you the ability to think how is this going to be built when you are designing something. On the right is me testing my engine I designed and built for the first time.
