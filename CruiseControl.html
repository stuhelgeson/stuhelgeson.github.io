Cruise Control


The first project in the second semester of my junior MEAM lab required you to build something with Arduino. This was quite fun having very little constraints and the ability to explore. My partner and I decided to make a remote controlled car that uses your iPhone as the remote control. We used a Red Bear Labs Bluetooth Low Energy shield to connect the Arduino controlled car and the iPhone. The car was built from scrap parts leftover from Robockey. My partner Casey put together a simple iPhone app that allows you to control the speed with a slider bar (this varies the PWM output from the Arduino), toggle the headlights with the press of a button, and steer using the data from the iPhoneâ€™s gyroscope and converting it to varying PWM values in the Arduino code to turn the two wheeled vehicle.
